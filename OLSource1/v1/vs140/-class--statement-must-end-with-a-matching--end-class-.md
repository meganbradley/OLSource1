# &#39;Class&#39; statement must end with a matching &#39;End Class&#39;
<CodeContentPlaceHolder>0\</CodeContentPlaceHolder> is used to initiate a <CodeContentPlaceHolder>1\</CodeContentPlaceHolder> block; hence it can only appear at the beginning of the block, with a matching <CodeContentPlaceHolder>2\</CodeContentPlaceHolder> statement ending the block. Either you have a redundant <CodeContentPlaceHolder>3\</CodeContentPlaceHolder> statement, or you have not ended your <CodeContentPlaceHolder>4\</CodeContentPlaceHolder> block with <CodeContentPlaceHolder>5\</CodeContentPlaceHolder>.  
  
 **Error ID:** BC30481  
  
### To correct this error  
  
-   Locate and remove the unnecessary <CodeContentPlaceHolder>6\</CodeContentPlaceHolder> statement.  
  
-   Conclude the <CodeContentPlaceHolder>7\</CodeContentPlaceHolder> block with a matching <CodeContentPlaceHolder>8\</CodeContentPlaceHolder>.
