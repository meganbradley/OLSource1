# &#39;End Class&#39; must be preceded by a matching &#39;Class&#39;
<CodeContentPlaceHolder>0\</CodeContentPlaceHolder> is used to complete a <CodeContentPlaceHolder>1\</CodeContentPlaceHolder> block; hence it can only appear at the end of the block. Either you have a redundant <CodeContentPlaceHolder>2\</CodeContentPlaceHolder>, or your <CodeContentPlaceHolder>3\</CodeContentPlaceHolder> statement appears outside the bounds of its corresponding <CodeContentPlaceHolder>4\</CodeContentPlaceHolder> block.  
  
 **Error ID:** BC30460  
  
### To correct this error  
  
-   Locate and remove any redundant <CodeContentPlaceHolder>5\</CodeContentPlaceHolder> statements.  
  
-   Move the <CodeContentPlaceHolder>6\</CodeContentPlaceHolder> statement to the appropriate location in your code.
